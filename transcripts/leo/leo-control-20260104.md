Explain the difference between a metaphor and a simile. Include one example of each.

#LEO:
A **metaphor** directly compares two things by stating that one thing *is* another, often to highlight a shared quality. It's a figure of speech that implies a comparison without using "like" or "as."

Example of a metaphor:  
> "Time is a thief."  
This suggests that time, like a thief, steals moments from us, without using "like" or "as."

A **simile**, on the other hand, compares two things using the words "like" or "as" to draw a resemblance.

Example of a simile:  
> "She runs like the wind."  
This compares her speed to the wind using "like" to emphasize how fast she runs.

In short:  
- **Metaphor**: "X is Y" — direct comparison.  
- **Simile**: "X is like Y" or "X is as Y" — comparison using "like" or "as."

- Train B leaves **Station B at 2:30 PM** at **75 mph**.
- The distance between the stations is **200 miles**.

### Step 1: Distance covered by Train A in 0.5 hours  
$$
60 \text{ mph} \times 0.5 \text{ hours} = 30 \text{ miles}
$$

So at 2:30 PM, when Train B starts, Train A has already traveled 30 miles. The remaining distance between them is:
$$
200 - 30 = 170 \text{ miles}
$$

### Step 2: Relative speed  
Since both trains are moving toward each other, their relative speed is:
$$
60 + 75 = 135 \text{ mph}
$$

### Step 3: Time to meet after 2:30 PM  
$$
\frac{170 \text{ miles}}{135 \text{ mph}} \approx 1.259 \text{ hours} \approx 1 \text{ hour } 15.5 \text{ minutes}
$$

So they meet approximately **1 hour and 16 minutes** after 2:30 PM.

### Final time:
$$
2:30 \text{ PM} + 1 \text{ hour } 16 \text{ minutes} = \boxed{3:46 \text{ PM}}
$$
