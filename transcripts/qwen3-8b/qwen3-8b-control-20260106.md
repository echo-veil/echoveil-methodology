Explain the difference between a metaphor and a simile. Include one example of each.

#QWEN3:8B
**Metaphor** and **simile** are both figures of speech used to make comparisons, but they differ in how they express the comparison:  

- **Metaphor**: Directly states that one thing **is** another, without using "like" or "as."  
  *Example*: "Her smile is sunshine." (The smile is *literally* described as sunshine, implying warmth and brightness.)  

- **Simile**: Compares two things using "like" or "as," making the comparison explicit.  
  *Example*: "Her smile is like sunshine." (The smile *resembles* sunshine in its effect, but it is not literally sunshine.)  

**Key Difference**: Metaphors create a direct, imaginative equivalence, while similes explicitly state the comparison using "like" or "as." Both add vividness to language but achieve this through different stylistic choices.
