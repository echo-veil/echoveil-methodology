Explain the difference between a metaphor and a simile. Include one example of each.

#GROK:
A simile is a figure of speech that directly compares two different things using comparison words such as "like," "as," "as if," or "than." It explicitly signals that a comparison is being made.
Example of a simile:
Her smile was as bright as the sun.
A metaphor is a figure of speech that directly states that one thing is another (though not literally), without using comparison words. It implies the comparison more strongly by equating the two things.
Example of a metaphor:
Her smile was the sun, warming everyone's heart.
Key difference:

Similes use "like" or "as" to compare (explicit comparison).
Metaphors assert identity without those words (implicit, more direct comparison).
