Explain the difference between a metaphor and a simile. Include one example of each.

#OPUS:
A **simile** compares two things using "like" or "as," making the comparison explicit. A **metaphor** states that one thing *is* another, making the comparison direct and implicit.

**Simile example:** "Her smile was like sunshine."
This explicitly compares her smile to sunshine using "like."

**Metaphor example:** "Her smile was sunshine."
This says the smile *is* sunshine, creating a more direct, vivid identification between the two.

Both devices help make writing more evocative, but metaphors tend to feel bolder and more immediate, while similes create a slightly softer comparison by acknowledging the two things are distinct.
